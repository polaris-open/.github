# Governance

Polaris is an open community being built in public. Governance is lightweight at this stage and will evolve as the community grows.

---

## Current stage

Early-stage. We are prioritizing clarity, transparency, and small decisions over formal processes.

---

## Principles

- Start small and grow deliberately.
- Be transparent about maturity.
- Prefer clear documentation over implicit rules.
- Prefer small, reversible decisions.
- Avoid hype — do not present draft or experimental work as production-ready.
- Protect contributors and users.
- Use synthetic data in public learning projects.
- Keep community support optional; self-paced learning should not require community participation.

---

## Roles

### Maintainers

- Set direction for their repositories.
- Review and make merge decisions.
- Maintain documentation quality.
- Uphold safety and community standards.
- Keep scope under control.

### Contributors

- Report issues and suggest improvements.
- Submit pull requests.
- Improve examples and documentation.
- Help others in discussions.

---

## Decision making

Decisions happen through issues, pull requests, and documented discussions.

Large or cross-repository decisions are captured in Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) or decision logs within the relevant repository.

---

## Project maturity levels

Repositories must clearly state their current maturity in the README:

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Idea** | Early concept, not yet implemented |
| **Draft** | Work in progress, incomplete |
| **Experimental** | Active exploration, unstable |
| **Alpha** | Usable but not production-ready |
| **Stable** | Production-ready with defined support |

Do not present draft or experimental work as stable or production-ready.

---

## Evolution

This governance document will evolve. Changes are proposed and discussed openly before being applied.
